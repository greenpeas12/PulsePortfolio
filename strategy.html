campaign strategy & optimization logic

strategic objective

the primary objective is early-stage engagement and trust formation rather than immediate conversion. given category saturation, pulse prioritizes emotional resonance and perceived understanding over feature differentiation.

target segmentation

  • primary: gen z students and early-career professionals experiencing burnout but skeptical of prescriptive self-improvement tools.
  • secondary: productivity-oriented users open to wellness, but resistant to motivational rhetoric.

strategic logic (insight → action)

  1. externalize blame: campaign messaging reframes burnout as a systemic condition rather than a personal failure, reducing user defensiveness and increasing receptivity.
  2. optimize onboarding for reassurance: early touchpoints emphasize emotional validation and adaptive intent before introducing functionality.
  3. signal transparency: marketing and product narratives expose simplified reasoning behind ai recommendations to build trust.

campaign roadmap (6 weeks)

  • weeks 1–2: awareness via short-form content centered on shared burnout narratives and social validation.
  • weeks 3–4: engagement through storytelling that introduces pulse as a responsive companion rather than a productivity tool.
  • weeks 5–6: activation using low-friction trials emphasizing personalization and adaptive support.

optimization framework

performance is evaluated using early indicators such as session depth, repeat visits, and onboarding completion rather than short-term conversion. messaging pivots are triggered by emotional disengagement signals rather than click-through decline alone.
